Twin-Guided Robotic Pavement Overlays (T-GRO)
— Safer, Faster, Cleaner Resurfacing

The vision is simple: what if DOTs could renew pavements overnight, keep traffic moving, and still deliver pay-factor quality – by pairing a live digital twin with robotic paving fleets that cut lane-closure hours roughly in half and reduce exposure and emissions at the same time?

Partner with Us

What We Enable

TwinPave Robotics Lab turns the ARPA-I award–winning T-GRO concept into a deployable platform: a continuously updated corridor digital twin that orchestrates semi-autonomous fleets.

Digital Twin–Guided Planning

2–72 hour corridor twins integrate traffic, geometry, plant production, and fleet telemetry to generate constructible, robot-ready overlay portfolios.

Goal: ≥ 40–50% fewer lane-closure hours

Robotic Paving Fleets

Coordinated 3D pavers, IC rollers, and ATMA/TMA vehicles operate as synchronized robotic work-cells under the twin, reducing on-foot exposure.

Goal: ≥ 30–50% fewer worker exposure hours

Twin-Linked QA & Optimization

Intelligent compaction, thermal maps, and IRI snapshots stream into the twin in real time, closing the loop between robotic execution and quality.

Goal: ≥ 1–2 pay-factor grade improvements

Cleaner, More Predictable Overlays

Optimized truck dispatch and rolling patterns from the twin cut idling, detours, and rework, delivering lower-carbon resurfacing.

Goal: ≥ 10–20% CO₂e reduction

Market Opportunity

From a business standpoint, the timing is incredible. The U.S. already spends about $140–150B per year on highways and streets, and about 30% of that is system preservation—overlays and rehab. That's a $40–50B resurfacing market today, growing toward $60–70B as pavements age and Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA)-level funding works its way through the system.

High-Value Focus

Our focus is on high-value corridors where lane closures are most expensive—interstates and urban arterials. That's roughly half the resurfacing spend, a $20–25B segment today.

Revenue Potential

TwinPave's twin-guided robotic orchestration is a software + robotics layer that can sit on top of these programs. If we power even 10–20% of resurfacing work on major corridors, that's a multi-billion-dollar annual revenue opportunity in the U.S. alone.

For Partners & Investors

Problem

Nighttime overlays create long closures, worker exposure, and inconsistent quality. Agencies lack a coordinated way to use data, automation, and robots to manage resurfacing as a system.

Solution

TwinPave combines corridor digital twins, generative scheduling, and robotic paving fleets into one platform that plans, executes, and verifies overlays with measurable gains in time, safety, and quality.

Traction

ARPA-I Stage 1 winner; active collaboration with DOT partners and OEMs on pilot corridors integrating ATMS data, IC/thermal telemetry, and supervised robotic work-cells.

Go-To-Market

Start with high-value pilot overlays under performance-based contracts, then scale via SaaS licensing and "T-GRO-ready" certifications for contractors and equipment manufacturers.
