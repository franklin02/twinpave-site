TwinPave Robotics Lab builds on the ARPA-I award-winning T-GRO concept: a continuously updated corridor digital twin that orchestrates semi-autonomous milling–paving–rolling–striping fleets. Our platform generates nightly overlay plans, evaluates traffic, safety, and carbon impacts, and drives robotic work-cells to execute overlays with fewer lane-closure hours and higher, more consistent quality.

What We Enable

Digital Twin–Guided Planning

2–72 hour corridor twins integrate traffic, geometry, plant production, and fleet telemetry to generate constructible overlay portfolios that agencies can trust and audit.

Goal: ≥ 40–50% fewer lane-closure hours.

Robotic Paving Fleets

Coordinated 3D pavers, IC rollers, and ATMA/TMA vehicles operate as synchronized work-cells, reducing on-foot exposure and improving density and smoothness.

Goal: ≥ 30–50% fewer worker exposure hours.

Data-Centric QA & Optimization

Intelligent compaction, thermal maps, and IRI snapshots feed directly into the twin, closing the loop between design, execution, and pay-factor quality.

Goal: ≥ 1–2 pay-factor grade improvements.

Cleaner, More Predictable Overlays

Optimized truck dispatch and rolling patterns cut idling, detours, and rework, delivering lower-carbon resurfacing and more reliable reopen times.

Goal: ≥ 10–20% CO₂e reduction.
